Almost exactly one year ago during my time at the blog factory, I gave a four-minute flash talk titled "Several Tips for Expanding Your Taste in Films Delivered at a High Speed," where I offered tips on how to broaden one's taste in movies.

A few days ago, while working on a completely different presentation (Design Systems), I found the slides and thought of sharing them with you here.

Keep a diary

Right after watching a movie, jot down the title, date, and a rating. It will help you to reflect on your movie experience and compare it with other movies you've seen.

{% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_07' -%}

Maintain a Watchlist

Write down interesting movies as you hear about them or every time someone recommends a movie to you. It will help you to avoid the decision paralysis.

{% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_09' -%}

Follow Movie Lovers

Get recommendations from friends with great taste, but also from your favorite filmmakers.

For example, every year Steven Soderbergh shares a list of everything he watched the previous year (here's the 2023 list). And if you like Ayo Edebiri you are in luck, because she shares what she has watched on her Letterboxd account.

{% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_11' -%}

Use Letterboxd

Letterboxd will tell in which of your streaming platforms a particular movie is available. You can also filter your watchlist by platform, follow other people's diaries, and watch their watchlist (which is both creepy and pretty useful)

You can follow me there if you like.

{% include photos/custom.html location='several-tips' filename='slide_13' -%} {% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_15' -%}

Go to the source

Explore films that inspired your favorite movies. For example, if you loved Mission Impossible, watch Topkapi, a heist film that inspired the famous wire scene and the TV show.

{% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_16' -%}

Become a completist

Transform your viewing experience into a game. For example, try watching as many films as possible from a specific director, or with a certain actor/actress, or within a genre, or set in a particular city.

Letterboxd offers a great feature called “Lists,” where you can create or follow lists that others have created. It also shows you how many films from those lists you've watched.

Join the Criterion Channel

Criterion Channel offers a great selection of classics and additional material, like interviews with the film crew or mini-docs. You'll need a VPN to create an account, but after that, they won't bother you again.

{% include photos/custom.html classname='has-margin-bottom' location='several-tips' filename='slide_25' -%}

Don't Avoid Bad Movies

Watching bad movies is inevitable, but they can broaden your taste. I often find myself talking more about bad movies and why they didn't work than about good movies.
